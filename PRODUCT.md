# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Campaign professionals** — digital directors, political operatives, campaign staff who need to track what AI tools their vendors, opponents, and the broader field are deploying. They check AICN to stay ahead of the competitive landscape, not to do academic research.

**Builders and vendors** — founders and product people building campaign technology who use AICN to monitor what's being adopted, what's being regulated, and where new opportunities or risks are emerging.

Both reader types are time-constrained, skeptical, and domain-fluent. They don't need AI explained to them. They need signal, fast.

## Product Purpose

AICN is a daily automated digest tracking AI use in political campaigns, elections, and issue advocacy. A Python + Claude pipeline gathers candidates from RSS feeds, broad web search, and a watchlist of tracked entities, then curates and summarizes them in a neutral, third-party voice. The result is published as a static site (GitHub Pages) and RSS feed, updated once per day.

Success looks like: campaign professionals and builders treating AICN as a standing resource — something they RSS-subscribe to, reference in briefings, or send to colleagues when a story breaks.

The automated methodology is not a caveat to be minimized; it's a feature. Open sourcing, showing sources, surfacing proposals, and making the pipeline legible are core to the product's credibility.

## Brand Personality

**Transparent infrastructure.** Precise, accountable, non-partisan. The design is closer to a well-sourced intelligence brief than a newsletter — it earns trust through methodology visibility, not editorial voice.

Three words: **calibrated · accountable · non-partisan**

Emotional goal: confidence that you're seeing the full picture, not a curated take.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS product marketing**: hero metrics, gradient accents, feature-grid layouts. AICN is a publication, not a product pitch.
- **AI-hype aesthetics**: glowing neural nets, circuit-board patterns, cyan-on-dark, anything that visually mimics the category AICN is covering. The site should feel credible to readers who are skeptical of AI.
- **Partisan political design**: red/blue energy, candidate-site aesthetics. AICN must read as third-party infrastructure across the political spectrum.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the pipeline.** The automated methodology is part of the brand identity. Sources, proposals, run timestamps, domain tracking — make them legible. Opacity would undermine the credibility the site depends on.

2. **Density is a feature.** Both reader types are time-constrained and domain-fluent. A scannable, information-dense layout is respectful. Scroll-padded hero sections and oversized whitespace signal that the content can't carry itself.

3. **Credibility through precision.** Every typographic and layout choice signals trustworthiness. No decorative moves that exist purely for aesthetics. The design earns trust by looking like it was built by someone who cares about accuracy.

4. **The curation is the product.** All design decisions serve the digest items and summaries — not the other way around. The chrome must frame the content, not compete with it.

5. **Third-party permanence.** The site should feel like infrastructure that will be running in five years. Trend-forward choices that feel dated in 18 months are wrong. Stability and durability are design values here.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the baseline. Good judgment on contrast, keyboard nav, and screen reader support. No known specific user needs. The reader profile skews toward desktop (checking news, not mobile-first workflows) but mobile must be usable.
